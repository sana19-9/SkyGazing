E V O L U T I O N - O F - E A R T H

The Earth has undergone a fascinating journey over the last 4.5 billion years, transforming from a molten mass to the diverse and vibrant planet we see today. This evolution encompasses a series of geological, atmospheric, and biological changes that shaped Earth's surface, climate, and ecosystems.

Approximately one-third the age of the universe, Earth formed by accretion from the solar nebula. Volcanic outgassing created the primordial atmosphere and oceans, but the early atmosphere contained almost no oxygen.

During its early stage, a giant impact collision with a planet-sized body named Theia is thought to have formed the Moon. Over time, the Earth cooled, forming a solid crust and allowing liquid water on its surface.

For more detailed information, visit the Wikipedia page on History Of Earth.
