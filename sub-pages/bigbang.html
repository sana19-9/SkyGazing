B I G - B A N G - T H E O R Y

The Big Bang Theory is the prevailing cosmological model explaining the origin and evolution of the universe. It posits that the universe began as an incredibly small, hot, and dense point roughly 13.8 billion years ago and has been expanding ever since. This model is supported by substantial scientific evidence from astronomy and physics.

Key Concepts: - The Beginning: Singularity, where space, time, matter, and energy were condensed into an infinitely small, dense state. - Expansion, Not Explosion: The "Big Bang" was a rapid expansion of space itself, cooling as it expanded, allowing fundamental particles to form.

Georges Lemaître proposed the concept of a "primeval atom" in 1931, which later became known as the Big Bang. Astronomer Edwin Hubble further supported the theory with his observations that galaxies are moving away from us, indicating the universe's expansion.

For more detailed information, visit the Wikipedia page on Big Bang.
